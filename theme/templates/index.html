What is Connascence?

Connascence
  1. The simultaneous birth of two or more things
  2. The act of growing at the same time
  3. (computing) A relationship between two or more elements of software in which changing one necessitates changing the others in order to maintain overall correctness.

Connascence is a software quality metric that describes different types of coupling. The concept of connascence is a taxonomy of specific types of coupling. Individual connaissances have three properties that are important to recognise:

Strength
The strength of a specific connascence is determined by how hard it is to change or remove. Generally, when refactoring code, changes should be made to prefer weaker types of connascence over stronger types.
Degree
The degree of a connascence refers to the degree of its occurance within a codebase. Connascences are often acceptable in small quantities, but should be removed if they occur frequently.
Locality
The locality of a connascence refers to how far apart the connascent elements are. Generally speaking, connascences will be more acceptible within a function or module, and less acceptible between modules.

Connascences are also split into static and dynamic connascences. Static connascences can be discovered by inspecting source code, while dynamic connascences can only be discovered by looking at the run-time behavior of a program. For this reason, static connascences are considered to be weaker than their dynamic counterparts.
